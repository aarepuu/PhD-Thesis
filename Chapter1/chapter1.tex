The use of data within communities and in civic action explores a wide range of disciplines from technical fields like sensing networks, pervasive computing and data systems to human computer interaction and computer-supported collaborative work. This chapter gives and overview of the increasing use of data in decision-making and the way citizens are involved in these data-driver processes.


\section{Participation in Smart City}
\label{section1.1}
The aim of a smart city is to merge information technology with city infrastructure to understand and address socioeconomic and environmental problems. There is a push for using data in all aspects of life –  to optimise and organise resources and improve sustainability but also to facilitate novel modes of participation, democracy and social interaction. Data exists or can be collected for just about everything, but it is often not accessible for everyone. Although there is an active effort by governments and other institutions to release datasets to improve services, there are a lot of barriers to effectively using data in civic participation and decision-making.
